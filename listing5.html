Places To Explore

Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites. One of the most renowned sites is the Khajuraho Group of Monuments, a collection of intricately carved temples dating back to the 10th and 11th centuries. These temples are famous for their exquisite sculptures depicting various aspects of human life, including sensuous and divine figures.

Manipur

Manipur is a state located in northeastern India, nestled among the foothills of the eastern Himalayas. It is known for its scenic landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant traditions. Manipur, often referred to as the "Land of Jewels," offers a unique blend of natural beauty, ethnic diversity, and artistic expressions. Imphal is the capital city of Manipur and serves as its cultural and administrative hub. The city is surrounded by picturesque hills and valleys, offering breathtaking views.

Chandigarh

Chandigarh is a union territory and capital city of two Indian states, Punjab and Haryana. It is a planned city located in the northern part of India, serving as a symbol of modern urban planning and design. Designed by renowned architect Le Corbusier, Chandigarh is known for its well-organized layout, beautiful architecture, and green spaces. Chandigarh is divided into several sectors, each planned to have its own residential, commercial, and recreational areas. The city's architectural landmarks, such as the Capitol Complex, Rock Garden, and Sukhna Lake, attract visitors from around the world.

Orissa

Odisha is a state located on the eastern coast of India, bordering the Bay of Bengal. It is known for its rich history, ancient temples, vibrant culture, and stunning natural landscapes.

Assam

Guwahati is the largest city in the northeastern state of Assam, India. It is situated on the banks of the Brahmaputra River and serves as the gateway to the enchanting landscapes and diverse cultures of the Northeast region. Guwahati is a bustling city with a blend of modern development and traditional charm.
