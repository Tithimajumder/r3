Uttarakhand

201 reviews

Uttarakhand, often referred to as the "Land of the Gods," is a state located in the northern part of India. It is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, spiritual significance, and adventure tourism. With Dehradun as its capital city, Uttarakhand is known for its majestic Himalayan peaks, pristine rivers, dense forests, and pilgrimage sites.

The state is home to some of the most famous Hindu pilgrimage destinations in India. The sacred shrines of Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri, collectively known as the Char Dham, attract thousands of devotees each year. These holy sites are nestled amidst stunning mountain landscapes and hold great religious and cultural significance.

Uttarakhand is blessed with diverse ecosystems and is often referred to as the "Land of the Rivers." The Ganges and its tributaries, such as the Yamuna and the Alaknanda, flow through the state, providing pristine water sources and offering opportunities for activities like river rafting and kayaking.
